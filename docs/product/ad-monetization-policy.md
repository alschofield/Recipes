# Ad Monetization Policy (Consumer Trust First)

This policy defines how ads can be introduced without degrading product quality or user trust.

## Principles

- Ads must never reduce recipe relevance or safety.
- Ads must be clearly disclosed.
- Ads must not interrupt critical user flows.
- Subscription value must remain clear (ad-light or ad-free options).

## Allowed Ad Formats

- Native sponsored recipe slots in browse/discovery contexts.
- Sponsored ingredient suggestions in planning contexts.
- Sponsored meal-plan collections with explicit labels.

## Disallowed Ad Formats

- Intrusive popups that block cooking/search workflows.
- Autoplay media in core recipe/detail pages.
- Ads that mimic organic results without disclosure.
- Ads tied to unsafe nutrition or cooking claims.

## Disclosure Requirements

- Use explicit labels: `Sponsored` or `Ad`.
- Keep disclosure visible in list and detail contexts.
- Distinguish paid placements from ranking logic.
- Link to a short explanation of ad policy in settings/help.

## Ranking and Quality Guardrails

- Organic relevance ranking remains primary.
- Sponsored items must pass baseline quality/safety checks.
- Cap sponsored density per page/session (start conservative).
- Never allow sponsorship to override allergen or safety constraints.

## UX Guardrails

- Avoid ad placements in the first-use activation flow.
- Avoid ads during active cooking steps and timers.
- Preserve page performance budgets (no heavy third-party scripts by default).
- Provide an ad-free benefit in paid tiers when feasible.

## Measurement and Rollout

- Track impact on activation, conversion, retention, and churn.
- Track trust signals (hides/reports, negative feedback).
- Launch as controlled experiments with rollback criteria.
- Roll back immediately if trust or retention materially drops.

## Compliance and Safety

- Ensure proper regional disclosure compliance.
- Maintain a sponsor category allowlist/denylist.
- Review claims for health/nutrition/legal risk before launch.
